Video Review Work

Some Context

In my spare time I produce and publish video reviews of various obscure role-playing games on my personal YouTube channel. Currently my channel has over 7, 000 subscribers and near 1 million total views. I also create reviews for the game news website RPGSite as a freelance reviewer. Creating a review entails that I record gameplay footage, script the reviews, record and edit voice over in Audacity. In addition various assets for text and titles are created in Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator as well as editing for all videos in Adobe After Effects.

ROLES: Audio Editing, Voice Over, Visual Effects Editor, Writer.

My Process.

I follow the same procedure for each review I create. This begins first by playing, then writing a critical analysis of a game, which serves as the script for a video review. Scripting involves researching the developmental history of each game, searching for release dates, as well as differences between different versions of the same game. The critical analysis focuses on the game's narrative, gameplay mechanics, graphics, sound and my overall enjoyment of the title.

Voice Over.

Once a script is finished, I then record my own voice over for the video review. This process involves, recording the vocal track, and editing it. Recording and editing are done in Audacity. Primarily editing consists of taking out breaths, recording mistakes and background noise.

Visual Effects Editing.

All video editing for my review work is done in Adobe After Effects. The primary editing includes syncing the voice over with gameplay footage of the game being reviewed. This ensures when elements of game are being discussed they are being displayed. Additionally, editing involves transitioning footage to one another, as well as creating effects such as text effects and transitions through key frame editing and motion graphics.
